# Community Engagement Guide: Congo Basin Forest Monitoring

## Introduction
Purpose of this guide, importance of community involvement in conservation.

## Module 1: Importance of Conservation
- Why forests matter: ecosystem services, climate regulation, biodiversity.
- Local benefits: clean water, soil stability, cultural values.
- Threats: deforestation, poaching, climate change.
- Role of community monitoring in protecting forests.

## Module 2: Species Identification
- Key wildlife species of the Congo Basin (elephants, gorillas, chimpanzees, okapis, etc.)
- Field signs: tracks, dung, nests, calls.
- Using identification cards and mobile apps.
- Safety around wildlife.

## Module 3: How to Use the Data Sheet
- Explanation of each column in the FIELD_DATA_SHEET.csv.
- Practical demonstration: filling out a paper sheet.
- Digital data entry using mobile devices (if applicable).
- Quality control: checking for completeness and accuracy.

## Module 4: Ethical Data Collection
- Respect for wildlife: minimum disturbance, no feeding, no chasing.
- Respect for local customs and private property.
- Confidentiality of sensitive data (e.g., exact locations of endangered species).
- Informed consent when involving other community members.

## Module 5: Safety Protocols
- Working in remote forest areas: buddy system, communication plans.
- Dealing with dangerous animals: avoidance strategies.
- Weather hazards: thunderstorms, extreme heat.
- Emergency procedures and contact numbers.

## Module 6: Communication Protocols
- How to report urgent findings (e.g., poaching activity) to authorities.
- Regular reporting schedule to project coordinators.
- Using radios, satellite phones, or mobile networks.
- Community meetings for sharing results and feedback.

## Module 7: Reporting Procedures
- Submitting completed data sheets (paper drop‑off, digital upload).
- Data validation and entry into central database.
- Generating simple summaries for community review.
- Using data for adaptive management decisions.

## Module 8: Community Benefits
- Direct incentives: stipends, equipment, training certificates.
- Indirect benefits: improved forest health, increased wildlife, enhanced local pride.
- Opportunities for leadership roles and skill development.
- Linking monitoring to eco‑tourism and other sustainable livelihoods.

## Module 9: Training Schedule
- Suggested 5‑day training workshop outline.
- Field practice sessions.
- Follow‑up refresher courses every 6 months.
- Mentorship program pairing experienced with new monitors.

## Module 10: Roles and Responsibilities
- Community Monitor: data collection, reporting, attending meetings.
- Local Coordinator: overseeing monitors, liaising with NGOs, organizing training.
- Steering Committee Representative: representing community interests at higher‑level meetings.
- Technical Support: providing equipment maintenance and data‑management assistance.
